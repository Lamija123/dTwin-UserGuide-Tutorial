5.11. Power Flow: Continuation Power Flow (CPF)

Task Overview:

This task implements a continuation power flow (CPF) method in a three-node network with:

  • Node 1 – Slack node (V₁ fixed in magnitude and angle)
  • Node 2 – Generator bus (PV node) with voltage regulation and reactive power limits
  • Node 3 – Load bus (PQ node) with increasing load injection S₃

Task Objective:

The objective is to investigate the power transfer limits and system voltage stability using CPF by gradually increasing the load at node 3 while monitoring voltage profiles and regulation mode transitions at node 2.

Program Analysis:

Model [type=NL domain=cmplx reInit=true name="PF With PV Regulating Generator And Its Limits"]:
  • reInit=true ensures reinitialization for each power flow point
  • S3_inj increases step-by-step via the Repeats block
  • Node 2 initially operates as a PV bus, regulating voltage to a setpoint of V₂ = 1.01 p.u.
  • Generator on node 2 has reactive limits: Qmin = -1 p.u., Qmax = 1.3 p.u.

The transition from PV to PQ mode occurs when the generator on node 2 reaches these reactive limits.

Interpretation

  • Up to around S₃ ≈ 2 p.u., the generator at node 2 successfully regulates the voltage, maintaining V₂ ≈ 1.01 p.u.
  • Once the reactive power demand exceeds the generator limit (Q > 1.3), the regulation is disabled and node 2 becomes a PQ bus.
  • Beyond this point, voltages on both nodes 2 and 3 decline steadily, indicating increased loading stress.
  • Near S₃ ≈ 5.3 p.u., numerical divergence and oscillations occur in v₂ and v₃, showing the proximity to a voltage collapse (nose point).

Conclusion:

The CPF analysis provides insight into the system’s ability to maintain voltage stability under increased loading conditions. The automatic transition of the generator at node 2 from PV to PQ mode due to reactive power limits plays a crucial role in the shape and stability margin of the PV curve. This approach is valuable in identifying loadability limits in planning and operation studies of power networks.
