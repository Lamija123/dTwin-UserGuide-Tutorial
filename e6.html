5.13. State estimation with Monte Carlo Loading

Task Overview:

This task expands the WLS-based state estimation model by including random noise in both power and voltage measurements. It leverages a Monte Carlo simulation approach to evaluate the robustness of the estimator and the statistical variability of the results under sensor noise.

Task Objective:

The aim is to simulate state estimation performance in the presence of stochastic measurement noise using multiple repetitions. This includes:

  • Gaussian noise added to active and reactive power injection at node 3,
  • Gaussian noise added to voltage magnitudes at all nodes,
  • Statistical analysis (min, max, mean, variance, standard deviation) of estimation errors,
  • Repetitive simulations to capture uncertainty across many trials.

Program Analysis:

Model [type=WLS domain=real eps=1e-5 name="GNSE With Noise and Statistics"]:

Distribution Blocks

  • g1 – Gaussian noise on P₃, Q₃ with std. dev. 0.03,
  • g2 – Gaussian noise on v₁, v₂, v₃ with std. dev. 0.04.

Monte Carlo Repetition Scheme

Each simulation step includes:

  1. Random noise generation on inputs,
  2. WLS estimation of v₂, v₃, φ₂, φ₃,
  3. Post-processing error computation: |P₃est − P₃real| and |Q₃est − Q₃real|,
  4. Aggregation of statistical indicators (min, max, average, variance, std. dev).

The simulation runs through nested loops with up to 20 × 10 = 200 total trials.

Figure 5.13.1: Monte Carlo simulation of voltages v2 and v3 under sensor noise

Observations

The voltage magnitudes at nodes 2 and 3 fluctuate around their nominal values (0.984 and 0.969 respectively). Despite the sensor noise, the variations remain bounded, with visibly larger spread observed at node 3 due to its higher electrical distance and sensitivity to injection variations.

Conclusion:

The Monte Carlo simulation confirms that the WLS estimator maintains stability under measurement noise, producing consistent voltage estimates. The use of statistical post-processing provides a quantitative measure of estimation uncertainty, making the model suitable for applications where sensor precision is limited.
