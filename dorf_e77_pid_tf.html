4.1. Dorf Example E7.7 (PID) with Transfer Functions (TF)

The problem that will be analyzed below is Example 7.7, which is found on page 452 of the book Modern Control Systems, by Richard C. Dorf and Robert H. Bishop.

This example considers the methodology for manual tuning of a PID controller within a closed-loop system. The focus is on demonstrating how to adjust the proportional term of the PID controller with the aim of achieving sustained oscillations in the system's response. This procedure represents an initial step in the process of manual PID parameter tuning, which can serve as a basis for further optimization of the controller's operation and the achievement of stable and precise control.

Task Overview

The assignment analyzes the effects of manually tuned PID controller parameters on the response of a closed-loop system that includes a third-order dynamic system. The regulator comprises proportional (P), integral (I), and derivative (D) components, which collectively work to minimize the difference between the reference (input) signal and the actual system output.

Task objective:

The objective of the assignment is to investigate the influence of the PID controller on the dynamic behavior of a third-order transfer function system, and to demonstrate the effectiveness of applying feedback in reducing static and dynamic errors, as well as in achieving the desired system output response.
